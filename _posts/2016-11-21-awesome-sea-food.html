    Sea-Treats are awesome, yummy and myriad in collection. We present to Thee the Unique Extraordinaire’ Stuff from around the Globe which You shall love to taste! Sea for yourself! Listed in no particular order :

  1. North American Seafood Chowder

    2. A stew made with celery, potatoes, onions, chicken broth, corn and flour - this delicious soup can stand in as a meal on its own right or could be a starter to your meal. Add wild monkfish to the stew and make the experience unique, tasty and slurpy. If you are in New York, make sure to try it out at The Lobster Place or The Home Restaurant - both at NYC. If you are a make-it-at-home and eat person, here are recipes to keep your Chowder-appetite satiated.

    1. The Super Ununsual Monkfish Chowder Recipe
    2. Curtis Stone’s Fish chowder recipe

  2. Mexican Fish Tacos

    3. Tacos comprise of a tortilla(thin flat bread) rolled or folded around a filling. This Mexican delicacy has cabbage,avocado, French shallot and White fillet rolled between Tortillas for exquisite taste. Served with Crema or Chilly Sauce, this is an absolute-must-try at Tacos Gardenias or Marisma Fish Tacos when you are at Mexico for the authentic Mexican taste. The recipe for the rest:

    1. Louis Keat’s Taco Recipe
    2. Marcela Valladolid’s Fish Taco Recipe

  3. Belgian Alcoholic Mussels

    4. This world-favourite meal has clam juice, lemon juice, mint and white wine as its ingredients in addition to mussels. If you’re a beer person, substitute beer instead of wine, to get beer braised mussels. A plain toasted bread and some chilled white wine go along splendidly with the meal. Chez Leon and De Nordzee in Brussels, Belgium are must-visit joints to enjoy tasty mussels when you’re in the country. For trying at home, here are the recipes you’ll want to know:

    1. Mussels in Tomato-Wine Broth
    2. Beer Braised Mussels

  4. Maryland Crab Cakes

    5. Crabmeat cooked with mayonnaise, egg, panko and onions to get fleshy crab cakes, complemented with remoulade made with shallots, parsley, mustard and capers - Yummy!! This mouth watering dish is famous in Maryland - particularly at Box Hill Pizzeria and Crab Cake Cafe. So, if you find yourself in Maryland, that’s where you should go for crabs! If you want to get all cheffy with the meal, here are the recipes you want to know!

    1. Crab Cakes with Spicy Remoulade
    2. Leslie’s Marland Crab Cake Recipe

  5. Malaysian Prawn Laksa

    6. Laksa is a Malay Noodle Soup, famous in Malaysia. Malaysian Prawn Laksa features chicken, coconut cream, brown sugar, lime, pineapple with prawns to delight your taste buds. Baba Low’s at Kuala Lampur and 7th Mile Kitchen at Petaling Jaya are preferred destinations when it comes to Malaysian Laksa cuisine, that you should visit when at Malaysia. For trying at home, you’ll want to try the following recipes:

    1. Michelle’s Prawn Laksa Recipe
    2. Chelsea Collin’s Prawn Laksa Recipe
